Brooklyn, NY

While in Rome, I knew I wanted to visit The Vatican. I was there right around when we got news of Pope Francis' illness, and got to see how the atmosphere was filled with hope for his recovery. Being here during the Jubilee year also meant I got to walk through The Holy Door. I have never considered myself to be a religious person, but I think there is something that goes beyond chance and fate that life led me here, then.

The Vatican, Vatican City

Morocco was another stop in my Spring Break itinerary. On our way from Marrakesh to Merzouga, we passed by Imouzzer. There was snow in the mountains—something I hadn’t expected at all. The contrast between the palm-lined valleys and the snow-capped peaks was surreal.

Imouzzer, Morocco

Another stop before reaching Marzouga was Ait Zineb. Christopher Nolan's The Odyssey was being produced. The set was still there. A part of me wanted to admire the scenery for what it was, set and all; the Cinema student in me spent more time in thought about Walter Benjamin's discussion of aura of the original, Jean Baudrillard's thoughts about the hyperreal, and André Bazin's ideas of ontology. My professors would be proud, as am I.

Ait Zineb, Morocco
